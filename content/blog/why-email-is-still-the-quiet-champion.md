Here is an interesting fact: the first marketing email was sent in 1978. Nearly fifty years later, with every new platform promising to replace it, email is still the channel that converts best and costs least.

## It is the only channel you own

Social algorithms change overnight. Ad costs climb every quarter. Your email list is yours. Nobody can throttle your reach to your own subscribers.

## Newsletters build trust before they build sales

I designed a veterinary clinic newsletter for a class project, and the brief shaped how I think about the channel. The goal was to advertise, educate, inform, build trust and create relationships. Notice that selling comes last.

A pet owner who receives genuinely useful dental care tips will remember the clinic when their dog needs an appointment. The newsletter earned that moment months in advance.

## Promotional emails work when they respect the reader

The other email I built was promotional: a wellness centre offer with a discount code and one clear button. The purpose of a promotional email is to encourage customers to take action and speed the buying process.

The discipline is restraint. One offer. One deadline. One button. Every extra element dilutes the decision you are asking the reader to make.

## Where I would start today

If a small business asked me where to invest first, I would say email before ads. Build the list slowly, send consistently, measure honestly. It is patient work in an impatient industry, and hospitality taught me that patient work is what customers remember.
