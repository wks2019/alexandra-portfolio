For a class project, I created a product detail page in Shopify based on a product specification sheet. The purpose was simple: increase awareness, drive traffic and increase conversions. Here is what I learned doing it.

## 1. The specification sheet is not the story

A spec sheet tells you the towel is a bamboo cotton blend, 600 GSM, available in three colours. A customer wants to know it stays soft after fifty washes and dries quickly in a small flat. The job is translation, not transcription.

## 2. One photo does the work of five paragraphs

I spent more time on imagery than copy, and it was the right call. People buy textiles with their eyes first. Show the texture, show the scale, show it in a real bathroom.

## 3. Reduce the clicks to purchase

Every extra step loses people. Clear price, visible payment options, one obvious button. My page went through three revisions and each one removed something.

## 4. Write the description for a scanner, not a reader

Short paragraphs. Bullet points for materials and care. Bold the two facts that matter most. Most visitors give you seconds, and the layout has to respect that.

## 5. Conversion starts before the page loads

The product title and preview image decide whether anyone arrives at all. I rewrote the title twice: once for humans, once more so search engines understood it too.

Small project, real lessons. The next one will be better, and that is the point.
